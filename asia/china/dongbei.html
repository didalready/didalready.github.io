BUDGET, STAY: VARIOUS HOSTELS

‍

Most people want to go to a warm place for Christmas to celebrate the holidays. Sunshine and sangrias and the like. Somewhere to spend the time with family (or, somewhere to escape them).

Thorin and I this year were 'relieved' of family duties and wanted somewhere cold for Christmas. Not only that, we wanted somewhere where Christmas wasn't a concept and New Years wasn't the big holiday for a fresh start. China sounded perfect. Scoot Airlines recently opened up a route to Dalian, and we were able to secure cheap flights round trip.

We packed our flimsy jackets Thorin took from London in preparation for the cold winter ahead in China. The plan was to start in Dalian, our port of arrival, then just work our way across Dong Bei (東北), which literally translates to the Northeastern part of China. It consists of Liaoning, Heilongjiang, and Jilin province.

This is our diary of the trip, including the cities we've been to and our impressions of them, and the stories we've gathered from the people we've met.
